Second Harmonic Generation (SHG)

Light

\[ E(t) = \mathrm{Re}\{ E(\omega) exp(i \omega t) \} \] \[ E(t) = \frac{1}{2}\left[ E(\omega) exp(i \omega t) + E^{*}(\omega) exp(- i \omega t)\right] \tag{2} \]

Substitute 2 in 1

\[ P_{\text{non-linear}} = P_{\text{non-linear}}(0) + \mathrm{Re}\left[ P_{\text{non-linear}}(2\omega) exp(i 2 \omega t) \right] \] \[ P_{\text{non-linear}} (0) = d E(\omega) E^{*}(\omega) \] \[ P_{\text{non-linear}} (2\omega) = d E^2(\omega) \]

\(2\omega\) is second Harmonic

Single frequency to double frequency in the function of \(2 \omega\)

Efficiency of SHG

✦
\(\eta\) = \(I(2\omega)\) \(I(\omega)\)
✦
\(\eta_{SHG}\) = \(c^2\) \(L^2\) \(A\) \(P\)
where
\(P\) - incident power
\(A\) - cross sectional area of interaction volume
L - interaction length
\(c\) - speed of light
We need high intensity to increase incident power, in pulsed laser, there are high intensities.
