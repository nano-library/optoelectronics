Some Frequency Generation (SFG)

Frequency Matching and Phase Matching

If a wave is electic field with function of omega \[ E(\omega_1) = A_1 \; exp(- j \; \overrightarrow{k_1} \; \overrightarrow{r}) \] \[ E(\omega_2) = A_1 \; exp(- j \; \overrightarrow{k_2} \; \overrightarrow{r}) \] \(exp(-j k r)\) is spatial part \[ P_{NL} (\omega_3) = 2 \; d \; E(\omega_1) \; E(\omega_2) = 2 \; d \; A_1 \; A_2 \; exp(- j \; \overrightarrow{k_3} \; \overrightarrow{r}) \] Frequency matching condition \[ \omega_1 + \omega_2 = \omega_3 \] Phase matching condition \[ \overrightarrow{k_1} + \overrightarrow{k_2} = \overrightarrow{k_3} \]
Medium acts as a light source of \(\omega_3 = \omega_1 + \omega_2\) with complex amplitude proportional to \(exp(- j \; \overrightarrow{k_3} \; \overrightarrow{r})\), where (\(\overrightarrow{k_3} = \overrightarrow{k_1} + \overrightarrow{k_2}\))
Argument of complex wave function is (\(\omega t - \overrightarrow{k} \cdot \overrightarrow{r}\)).
(\(\omega_1 + \omega_2 = \omega_3\)) ensures both temporal and spatial phase matching. physical meaning is to sustain mutual interaction over extended duration of time and region of space.

Three Wave Mixing Modulities, Parameteric interaction process

Phase matching \[ \omega_3 = \omega_1 + \omega_2 \] once omega3 is generated, it interacts with omega1 or omega2, then due to this interaction omega2 or omega1 is generated while maintaining phase matching. \[ \omega_2 = \omega_3 - \omega_2 \] omega 3 and omega 2 generates omega1

Optical Frequency Conversion

Up conversion

\[ \omega_3 = \omega_1 + \omega_2 \]
SHG is a degenerate special case of SFG. If \(\omega_1 = \omega_2 = \omega\), then \(\omega_3 = 2 \; \omega\)

Down conversion

\[ \omega_2 = \omega_3 - \omega_1 \]

Applications

  • to generate coherent light at wavelength where no adequete lasers are available.
  • optical mixers in optical communication system.

Optical Parameteric Amplifiers (OPA)

When Wave 1 interacts with wave 3, wave 1 is enchanced, wave 2 is created. Pump is wave 3, signal is wave 1. wave 2 is idler. This device operates as a coherent amplifier at frequency \(\omega_1\). Gain depends on power of pump wave.

Applications

  • Amplifing the signal
  • Helps in detecting small signals by amplifying them.

Optical Parameteric Oscillator (OPO)

Optical Parametric Amplifier + Proper Feedback is OPO. Only pump wave is supplied.

Applications

  • Used for generation of coherent light.
  • Mode locked pulse trains over a continuous range of frequency.
  • If there is no tunable laser, we can use OPOs.

Spontaneous Parametric Down Conversion (SPDC)

Only input - pump that is wave 3. Down conversion to lower frequency wave 1 and wave 2. \[ \omega_1 + \omega_2 = \omega_3 \\ k_1 + k_2 = k_3 \] leads to multiple solutions. Each forming a pair of wave 1 and wave 2, with specific frequency (\(\omega\)) and direction (k).
