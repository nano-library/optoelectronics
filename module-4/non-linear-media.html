✨ Are all mediums linear? No. A medium may be linear orNon-linear, dielectric or conducting, isotropic or anisotropic.
13.1

Linear Optical Medium

Intensity Independence
The refractive index \(n\) and loss coefficient \(\alpha\) are independent of light intensity.
Superposition
The principle of superposition applies, fields add without interaction.
Frequency Preservation
The frequency of light is never altered by passing through the medium.
Core Limitation
Beams in the same region have no effect on each othe, light cannot be used to control light.
13.2

Non-linear Optical Medium - High Intensity Behaviour

Intensity-Dependent Properties
The refractive index \(n\) and speed of light \(v\) are no longer constant, both become functions of light intensity.
Superposition Breaks Down
The principle of superposition is violated, fields no longer simply add.
Frequency Shifts
The frequency of light is altered as it passes through the medium, the color of light itself changes.
Light Controls Light
Photons now interact. Unlike linear media, light can be used to control light.
13.3

The Role of the Non-linear Optical Medium

Non-linearity is Not in Free Space
Non-linear behavior is never observed when light travels in free space, non-linearity resides in the interaction between light and medium.
Medium as Mediator
Light does not interact with light directly, the non-linear medium mediates the interaction.
Field Modifies Medium, Medium Modifies Field
An optical field alters the properties of the medium, which in turn modifies another optical field, or even the original field itself.
13.4

Dielectric vs Non-linear Optical Medium

Dielectric Medium
Non-linear Medium
Dispersive

Velocity depends on frequency, different frequencies travel at different speeds through the medium.

Non-Dispersive

Velocity does not depend on frequency, all frequencies propagate at the same speed through the medium.

Homogeneous

Properties are uniform throughout, the medium looks the same at every point in space.

Non-Homogeneous

Properties vary from point to point, the medium is not uniform throughout its structure.

Isotropic

Properties are independent of direction, the medium responds the same regardless of how light is oriented.

Isotropic

Like the dielectric medium, it remains direction-independent in its response to light.

13.5

Second-Order Non-linearity - Applications

✦ Equation (14.1)
\(\vec{P}\) \(\propto\) \(|\vec{E}|^2\)
Second Harmonic Generation
A monochromatic wave is frequency-doubled as it passes through the medium, the output is at twice the input frequency.
Sum & Difference Frequency Generation
Two monochromatic waves mix to produce a third wave at either their sum \((\omega_1 + \omega_2)\) or difference \((\omega_1 - \omega_2)\) frequency.
Parametric Amplification
Two monochromatic waves interact within the medium to amplify a third wave, energy is transferred between fields.

Third-Order Non-linearity - Applications

✦ Equation (14.2)

Third Harmonic Generation
A monochromatic wave is frequency-tripled as it passes through the medium, the output is at thrice the input frequency.
Self Phase Modulation
Intensity-dependent refractive index of a medium causes a propagating optical pulse to modulate its own phase.
Self Focusing
Four Wave Mixing
Phase Conjugation
