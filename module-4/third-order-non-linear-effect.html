Self phase modulation (SPM)

Phase shift measured by an optical beam of power \(P\) and cross section \(A\) travelling a distance \(L\) in the Medium \[ \phi = n(I) \cdot \left(\frac{2\pi}{\lambda}\right)L \] \[ \phi = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda} \left[ n + n_2 I \right]L \] \[ \phi = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda} \left[ n + n_2 \frac{P}{A} \right]L \] \[ \Delta \phi = \frac{2 \pi n_2 L P}{\lambda A} \] \[ L = 1m, A = 10^{-2} mm^2, n_2 = 10^{-10} cm^2/W, P = 0.5, \Delta \phi = \pi, \lambda = ? \] \[ \lambda = \frac{ \Delta \phi A}{2 \pi n_2 L P} \] \[ \lambda = \frac{A}{2 n_2 L P} \] \[ \lambda = 1 \mu m \]

Self Focusing

Phase modulation can be converted to intensity modulation. Weak Optical beam may be controlled by an intense optical beam. Associated with self phase modulation, intensity beam transmitted through or shift of non-linear material. refractive index change mimics the intensity pattern. If beam has its highest intensity at the centre. maximum change in refractive index is also at the centre. Non-linear sheet then acts as a graded index medium. imports to the wave a non uniform phase shift. causing wave front curvature. medium can act as a lens with power dependent focal length.

Spatial Soliton

Occurs in the time doain when group velocity dispersion is compensated by SPM. if it is time, it is called temporal, if it is space, it is called spatial. Intense optical beam travels through substantial thickness of non-linear medium. Refractive index is altered non-uniformly. Medium acts as a graded index wave guide, maximum at maxima. beam can create its own wave guide. If the intensity of the beam has same spatial distribution in the transverse plane as one of the modes of the wave guide, that the beam itself creates. beam propagates self consistently without changing its spatial distribution. diffraction is compensated by self phase modulation (SPM). self guided beam, that is called, spatial solitons.
